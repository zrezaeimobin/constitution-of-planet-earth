## 2. Full Legal Text

### Preamble

We, the People of Planet Earth, in acknowledgment of the blessings of life granted by the natural world, and in recognition of our responsibility to preserve these blessings for ourselves, our posterity, and all generations yet to come, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the governance, protection, and enduring peace of the human family and the Earth which sustains it.

---

### Article I â€” Legislative Power

**Section 1. The Legislative Authority**  
All legislative powers herein granted shall be vested in a Parliament of Earth, consisting of a House of Humanity and a House of Nations.

**Section 2. The House of Humanity**  
1. The House of Humanity shall be composed of Members chosen directly by the People of the Earth, apportioned by population, elected every fourth year by universal suffrage.  
2. No Person shall be a Member who has not attained the age of twenty-five years and been a resident of their region for at least three years.  
3. The House shall choose its Speaker and other Officers; and shall have the sole power of Impeachment.

**Section 3. The House of Nations**  
1. The House of Nations shall be composed of representatives appointed by each sovereign state or recognized territory, each allotted an equal vote.  
2. Members shall serve six-year terms, with procedures of selection left to each nation.  
3. The House of Nations shall elect a Presiding Officer but shall not appoint any executive of the Earth Government.

**Section 4. Legislative Powers**  
The Parliament shall possess power:

1. To enact laws for the preservation of life, liberty, and the stability of the planet;  
2. To regulate commerce among nations, regions, and peoples;  
3. To establish uniform environmental protections and standards;  
4. To levy taxes and allocate resources for the common defense of Earthâ€™s ecosystems;  
5. To declare a State of Environmental Emergency;  
6. To create courts inferior to the Supreme Tribunal;  
7. To define crimes of planetary harm and provide for their punishment;  
8. To admit new territories or peoples into representation;  
9. To enact all laws necessary for executing the powers vested herein.

**Section 5. Limitations on Legislative Power**  
The Parliament shall pass no law:

1. Establishing any exclusive state religion;  
2. Abridging the freedom of speech, thought, or inquiry;  
3. Permitting slavery, forced labor, or human trafficking;  
4. Allowing the wanton destruction of nature or ecosystems;  
5. Targeting individuals or groups through bills of attainder;  
6. Suspending fundamental rights except in times of extreme planetary peril, and then only by two-thirds concurrence of both Houses.

---

### Article II â€” Executive Power

**Section 1. The Executive Authority**  
The Executive Power shall be vested in a Guardian-President of Earth. The Guardian-President shall hold office during a term of six years and shall be eligible for re-election only once.

**Section 2. Qualifications**  
No Person shall be eligible who:

1. Has not attained the age of thirty-five years;  
2. Has not resided on Earth for at least fifteen years;  
3. Has not sworn an oath to preserve the planet and the life upon it.

**Section 3. Powers and Duties**  
The Guardian-President shall:

1. Faithfully execute the laws enacted by Parliament;  
2. Serve as Commander of the Earth Guard for environmental protection;  
3. Make treaties, provided two-thirds of the House of Nations concur;  
4. Nominate Judges of the Supreme Tribunal;  
5. Convene either House on extraordinary occasions;  
6. Ensure that the environment, climate, and life-support systems of the planet are preserved from harm;  
7. Deliver to Parliament a yearly Report on the State of the Earth.

**Section 4. Removal from Office**  
The Guardian-President, Vice-Guardian, and all civil officers shall be removed from office on impeachment for, and conviction of: treason against humanity or nature, bribery, high crimes, crimes of planetary harm, or gross negligence resulting in environmental catastrophe.

---

### Article III â€” Judicial Power

**Section 1. Judicial Authority**  
The judicial power shall be vested in one Supreme Tribunal of Earth and in such inferior courts as Parliament may establish.

**Section 2. Jurisdiction**  
The judicial power shall extend to:

1. All cases arising under this Constitution;  
2. Disputes between nations, peoples, or regions;  
3. Crimes against humanity;  
4. Crimes against nature and the planetary life-support system;  
5. Cases involving the rights of future generations;  
6. Cases relating to treaties, agreements, and planetary governance.

**Section 3. Tenure**  
Judges of the Supreme Tribunal shall hold their offices during good behavior and may only be removed by impeachment.

**Section 4. Environmental Precedence**  
In all cases where human interests conflict with the survival of planetary life-support systems, the Tribunal shall assign precedence to the preservation of Earth, insofar as such preservation safeguards the welfare of present and future generations.

---

### Article IV â€” Protection of Life & Nature

**Section 1. The Right of the Planet to Endure**  
The Earth, as the foundation of all life, shall possess the inherent right to exist, regenerate, and maintain the natural systems upon which humanity depends.

**Section 2. Prohibited Acts**  
No person, corporation, or nation shall:

1. Cause severe or irreversible harm to essential ecosystems;  
2. Pollute air, water, or soil in a manner that endangers human or non-human life;  
3. Destroy biodiversity through unchecked exploitation;  
4. Engage in practices that render land uninhabitable for future generations.

**Section 3. Environmental Stewardship**  
Parliament shall enact laws:

1. Regulating the extraction of natural resources;  
2. Protecting oceans, rivers, forests, and wildlife;  
3. Establishing planetary emissions standards;  
4. Preventing catastrophic climate alteration;  
5. Upholding intergenerational justice.

**Section 4. Duty to Restore**  
Nations and persons who cause significant environmental harm shall, by law, be compelled to restore or compensate for such damage, according to measures set forth by Parliament.

---

### Article V â€” Rights of Humanity

**Section 1. Fundamental Rights**  
All persons on Earth shall possess the following rights:

1. The right to life, dignity, and personal security;  
2. The right to clean air, potable water, and safe food;  
3. The right to freedom of thought, expression, and belief;  
4. The right to education, health, and knowledge;  
5. The right to fair treatment before the law;  
6. The right to inhabit a safe and stable climate.

**Section 2. Equality of Persons**  
No law shall abridge the equality of persons based on sex or gender, ethnicity or origin, belief or non-belief, disability, age, wealth or poverty, or any other condition of birth or circumstance.

**Section 3. Protection of Women & Mothers**  
Recognizing the unique biological role of women in bearing life, the law shall ensure:

1. Protection during pregnancy and childbirth;  
2. Freedom from violence, exploitation, and coercion;  
3. Equal opportunity in all spheres of life;  
4. That no woman shall be compelled into duties beyond her physical capacity.

**Section 4. Protection of Children**  
Children shall be entitled to:

1. Safety, nourishment, and education;  
2. Freedom from exploitation and labor;  
3. A stable climate and clean environment;  
4. Protection from neglect, trafficking, and abuse.

**Section 5. Protection of Elders**  
Elders shall be afforded dignity, care, and support consistent with their vulnerability and contributions to society.

---

### Article VI â€” Rights of Nature

**Section 1. Recognition of Non-Human Life**  
The natural world â€” including animals, forests, rivers, oceans, and ecosystems â€” shall possess fundamental rights:

1. The right to exist and flourish;  
2. The right to protection from cruelty and destruction;  
3. The right to restoration when harmed;  
4. The right to humane treatment.

**Section 2. Duties Toward Animals**  
Parliament shall enact laws ensuring humane treatment of animals and preventing needless suffering, while respecting sustainable human needs.

**Section 3. Legal Standing of Nature**  
Ecosystems and species shall have standing in the courts through appointed guardians empowered to defend their rights.

**Section 4. Sacredness of Biodiversity**  
The extinction of species is hereby recognized as an injury to present and future generations and shall be prevented through appropriate measures of conservation.

---

### Article VII â€” Duties of Persons

**Section 1. General Obligations**  
Every person shall:

1. Refrain from harming human life or the planet;  
2. Act with regard for the welfare of others;  
3. Uphold honesty and fairness in dealings;  
4. Support the preservation of the planet's life-support systems.

**Section 2. Civil Responsibilities**  
Citizens shall:

1. Comply with the laws established herein;  
2. Respect the rights and freedoms of others;  
3. Contribute to the protection of the environment;  
4. Participate, when able, in public decision-making.

**Section 3. Obligation to Future Generations**  
All persons are bound by moral and civic duty to preserve Earth for those yet unborn, and to abstain from acts that jeopardize their survival or freedom.

### Article VIII â€” Peace & Harmony

**Section 1. Prohibition of Aggressive War**  
No nation or entity shall wage war for conquest, profit, or revenge. War shall be permitted only in defense of life or in response to genocide, mass atrocity, or environmental destruction threatening humanity.

**Section 2. Diplomacy First**  
All disputes among nations shall first be addressed through diplomacy, mediation, or judgment of the Supreme Tribunal.

**Section 3. Weapons of Mass Harm**  
The development, deployment, or use of weapons whose destructive capacity endangers the planet or humanity as a whole is prohibited.

**Section 4. Crimes Against Peace**  
Acts of aggression, genocide, ecocide, and mass destruction shall be tried before the Supreme Tribunal as crimes against the Earth and humanity.

---

### Article IX â€” Knowledge & Future Generations

**Section 1. Advancement of Knowledge**  
Parliament shall promote the progress of science, medicine, environmental stewardship, and the useful arts, for the benefit of humanity and the planet.

**Section 2. Protection of Research**  
No law shall impede the free pursuit of knowledge, provided such pursuit does not inflict harm upon human life, non-human life, or the stability of the Earth.

**Section 3. Education**  
All persons shall have access to education sufficient to:

1. Understand and participate in civic life;  
2. Care for themselves and their communities;  
3. Comprehend their relationship to the natural world;  
4. Pursue knowledge and skills for the betterment of society.

**Section 4. Future Generations**  
In all legislative, executive, and judicial actions, consideration shall be given to the rights and welfare of future generations, who shall not be deprived of a habitable planet or the means of survival.

---

### Article X â€” Transparency & Accountability

**Section 1. Transparency of Governance**  
The records, debates, and decisions of all branches of planetary governance shall be made accessible to the public, except where secrecy is necessary for the immediate security of humanity.

**Section 2. Anti-Corruption Measures**  
Parliament shall enact laws preventing corruption, undue influence, and the concentration of power contrary to the public good.

**Section 3. Ethical Conduct**  
All public officers shall act with integrity, impartiality, and fidelity to this Constitution.

**Section 4. Right to Petition**  
The people shall have the right peaceably to assemble, petition for redress of grievances, and seek reform of governmental actions.

---

### Article XI â€” Amendments

**Section 1. Proposal of Amendments**  
The Parliament may propose amendments to this Constitution whenever two-thirds of both Houses deem it necessary.

**Section 2. Ratification**  
Amendments shall become valid as part of this Constitution when ratified by three-fourths of the nations represented in the House of Nations, or by conventions in three-fourths thereof.

**Section 3. Restrictions**  
No amendment shall:

1. Abolish the right to clean air, water, and a stable climate;  
2. Permit slavery or crimes against humanity;  
3. Undermine the rights of nature;  
4. Concentrate unlimited power in a single person or body;  
5. Endanger the survival of future generations.

---

### Article XII â€” Supremacy

**Section 1. Supreme Law**  
This Constitution, and the laws and treaties made under its authority, shall be the supreme law of Earth; and the judges in every nation shall be bound thereby.

**Section 2. National Laws**  
No national constitution or law shall contravene the rights herein established or diminish protections afforded to humanity or nature.

**Section 3. Conflicts**  
When a conflict arises between human interest and planetary survival, the preservation of the Earth shall prevail insofar as it ensures the long-term welfare of all life.

---

### Article XIII â€” Ratification

**Section 1. Ratification and Commencement**  
This Constitution shall take effect when ratified by the nations and peoples of Earth according to procedures agreed upon by their representatives.

**Section 2. Continuity**  
All existing institutions consistent with this Constitution shall remain in force until replaced or reformed by laws enacted under its authority.
